Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ), known as the "gentle art" is a combat sport that focuses on ground fighting and submission grappling. It emphasizes technique and leverage, allowing a smaller, weaker person to defend themselves against a larger opponent in a gentle manner.

History of BJJ

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) traces its roots to Japanese Jujutsu and Judo, which were introduced to Brazil in the early 20th century. In the 1920s, a Japanese judoka named Mitsuyo Maeda migrated to Brazil and began teaching martial arts. One of his students, Carlos Gracie, and Carlos' younger brother Hélio adapted and further developed these techniques, eventually founding Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. The Gracie family played a pivotal role in refining and popularizing BJJ, organizing competitions to showcase its effectiveness. Why should you do BJJ?

  1. Full-Body Workout:

    BJJ is a full-body workout that enhances strength, flexibility, and cardiovascular fitness. The constant movement, grappling, and sparring engage various muscle groups, promoting overall physical well-being.

  2. Practical Self-Defense:

    BJJ is a martial art with a strong focus on practical self-defense. The techniques taught in BJJ empower individuals to defend themselves in real-life situations.

  3. Mental Acuity and Discipline:

    The mental aspect of BJJ is equally important. It's like a physical chess game, requiring strategic thinking, problem-solving, and quick decision-making. Learning and implementing intricate techniques foster mental acuity and discipline.

  4. Inclusivity:

    BJJ is inclusive and adaptable. Practitioners of all ages, sizes, and fitness levels can participate and benefit. It's not about overpowering opponents but using technique and leverage effectively, making it an art accessible to a diverse range of people.

  5. Community and Camaraderie:

    The BJJ community often emphasizes respect, humility, and camaraderie. It's a sport that encourages mutual growth and learning, creating a supportive environment for practitioners to develop not only as athletes but also as individuals.

Belt System

Color Skill Level Average Time To Attain This Belt from Start Requirements
White Beginner, focuses on fundamentals and basic techniques. Starting Belt Attendance, understanding basic positions, and escapes.
Blue Intermediate, develops a more extensive technical knowledge and understanding of BJJ strategies. Varies widely, typically 1 to 2 years. Demonstrated proficiency in fundamental techniques, ability to apply them in sparring, understanding basic submissions and escapes.
Purple Advanced, emphasizes refinement of techniques, development of personal style, and increased sparring experience. 3 to 5 years. In-depth knowledge of a broad range of techniques, ability to chain techniques together, teaching skills, competition experience.
Brown High-level, mastery of techniques, deep understanding of strategy, and preparation for black belt. 5 to 7 years. High-level technical proficiency, consistent success in competition, teaching and mentoring skills, contribution to the BJJ community.
Black Expert, signifies a high level of technical proficiency, teaching ability, and a deep understanding of the art. Degrees indicate further experience and contributions. 7 to 10 years. Exceptional technical skill, extensive competition success, teaching ability, contribution to the BJJ community.
